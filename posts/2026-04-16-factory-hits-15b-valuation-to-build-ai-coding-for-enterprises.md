AI-assisted coding continues to dominate as one of the most valuable applications of generative artificial intelligence. As competition intensifies among major players, a new startup, Factory, is emerging as a strong contender in the enterprise AI coding space.

The company has announced a significant funding milestone, highlighting growing investor confidence in AI-driven software development tools.

---

## Funding and Valuation

Factory recently raised $150 million in a new funding round, bringing its valuation to $1.5 billion.

**Key details:**

- Funding round led by Khosla Ventures  
- Participation from Sequoia Capital, Insight Partners, and Blackstone  
- Keith Rabois (Khosla Ventures) joined the company’s board  

This investment reflects strong belief in the future of AI-powered development tools for enterprises.

---

## The Growing AI Coding Market

AI coding tools have become one of the most widely adopted use cases for artificial intelligence.

The market already includes major competitors such as:

- Anthropic (Claude Code)  
- Cursor  
- Cognition  

Despite this competition, investors believe there is still room for new players offering differentiated solutions.

---

## What Factory Is Building

Factory is focused on developing AI agents specifically designed for enterprise engineering teams.

**Core capabilities include:**

- Assisting developers in writing and managing code  
- Supporting large-scale software development workflows  
- Integrating with enterprise systems and processes  

The company aims to improve productivity while reducing the complexity of modern software development.

---

## Key Differentiator: Multi-Model Flexibility

One of Factory’s main advantages is its ability to work across multiple AI models.

According to founder Matan Grinberg, the platform can switch between different foundation models, including:

- Anthropic’s Claude  
- Other emerging AI systems such as DeepSeek  

This flexibility allows enterprises to choose the best model for specific tasks rather than relying on a single provider.

---

## Enterprise Adoption

Factory has already gained traction among major organizations.

**Notable customers include:**

- Morgan Stanley  
- Ernst & Young  
- Palo Alto Networks  

This early adoption suggests strong demand for AI tools tailored to enterprise-level development needs.

---

## Company Background

Factory was founded in 2023 by Matan Grinberg, who was a PhD student at UC Berkeley at the time.

The company’s early momentum began after Grinberg connected with Sequoia Capital partner Shaun Maguire through a cold email. Their shared academic interests helped establish the foundation for the startup’s growth.
