As artificial intelligence systems become more complex, ensuring their reliability has emerged as a critical challenge for enterprises. InsightFinder, a company focused on AI-driven observability, is positioning itself to solve this problem with a new approach to monitoring and diagnosing intelligent systems.

The company has announced a $15 million Series B funding round, signaling growing investor interest in tools that help businesses manage the risks associated with AI agents.

---

## The Shift in Observability

Observability tools have traditionally focused on tracking system performance and identifying failures. However, the rapid adoption of AI agents has introduced new layers of complexity.

Modern enterprises now need to:

- Monitor not just infrastructure, but also AI models and data pipelines  
- Understand how different components interact within a system  
- Control rising operational costs while maintaining reliability  

This shift has created demand for more advanced, AI-aware observability solutions.

---

## About InsightFinder

InsightFinder is built on over 15 years of academic research in machine learning and system analytics.

Since its founding, the company has focused on:

- Detecting anomalies in IT infrastructure  
- Diagnosing root causes of system failures  
- Proactively resolving issues before they escalate  

With the rise of AI agents, InsightFinder is now expanding its capabilities to address the unique challenges of autonomous systems.

---

## New Focus: AI Agent Reliability

The company’s latest platform aims to handle the full lifecycle of AI system monitoring.

**Core capabilities include:**

- Detection of anomalies across systems  
- Diagnosis of complex, multi-layer issues  
- Automated remediation of problems  
- Prevention of future failures  

This end-to-end approach is designed to ensure that AI agents operate reliably within enterprise environments.

## Leadership and Funding

InsightFinder was founded by Helen Gu, a computer science professor at North Carolina State University with prior experience at IBM and Google.

The company recently raised $15 million in a Series B funding round led by Yu Galaxy.

This investment will likely be used to expand product development and scale adoption among enterprise customers.

## The Core Industry Challenge

According to Helen Gu, the biggest issue facing organizations today is not just identifying where AI systems fail, but understanding why they fail within a broader ecosystem.

Key challenges include:

- Distinguishing between model issues and data issues  
- Identifying infrastructure-related bottlenecks  
- Analyzing interactions across the entire technology stack  

In many cases, failures are not caused by a single component but by the interaction between multiple layers.

As AI adoption accelerates, reliability becomes a major concern for businesses.

Solutions like InsightFinder aim to address these challenges by providing deeper insights and automated problem-solving capabilities.
